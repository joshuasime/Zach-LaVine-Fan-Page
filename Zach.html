Childhood

Zach was born in Renton, Washington to his father Paul LaVine and CJ LaVine. Paul played professional football in the NFL and CJ played softball. Paul pushed Zach throughout his young life to be the best player he could be, by his senior year at Bothell High School he was 6ft 3in and averaging 28.5 ppg, 3.4 rpg, and 2.5 apg. He was named Washignton player of the year and Mr. Basketball. He played in the Ballislife All-American Game and won the Ballislife slam dunk contest. He was listed as a four star recruit and the No. 12 Shooting Guard and No. 44 overall player in the Nation.







College Years

LaVine committed to UCLA June 20, 2012, nearly going to the University of Washington instead. LaVine started the 2013-14 season as the team's sixth man, where he show cased his shooting and dunking skills attracting lots of attention from NBA scouts. LaVine averaged 9.4 points per game throughout the season and was voted Pac-12 All freshman team. LaVine then declared for the NBA draft on April 16, 2014 despite having 3 years of college eligibility left, signing with Nima Namakian of BDA sports.


Minnesota Timberwolves

LaVine was selected 13th overall in the NBA draft on june 26, 2014. LaVine started coming off the bench for the fist 5 games of the 2014-15 season until the starting point gaurd Ricky Rubio went out with an ankle injury. However this was short lived with Coach Flip Saunders making vet Mo Williams the starter. LaVine had 28 points in a win over the Lakers to make him the second ever teenager with 25+ points and five assists. LaVine was then shortly moved back into the starting position after spasms sidelined Mo Williams. LaVine had his first double-double against the Spurs with 22 points and 10 assists becoming the fourth teenager to record a 20 point 10 assist game. Ricky Rubio returned later in the season resulting in less minutes for LaVine however Mo Williams was also traded in the same month.

LaVine wet on the win the 2015 NBA Slam dunk contest with his first two dunks earning a perfcet 50 for the first time since Dwight Howard in 2009. Lavine also played in the Rising Stars game ending a exceptional All Star weekend for him. Zach went on to be named to the All NBA Rookie Second team averaging 10.1 points for the year.

Coming into the 2015-2016 season the Timberwolves exercised their third-year tea option on LaVine's rookie contract. Ricky Rubio was side lined early on in the season making LaVine the starter once again. Zach, Karl-Anthony Towns and Andrew Wiggins became the first trio of NBA teammates under 21 to score at least 20 points in the same game. During the 2016 All Star Rising star game LaVine scored 30 points earning him the Rising Star MVP, and participated in the 2016 Dunk Contestin the most electrifying dunk contest in years with a controversal win over Aaron Gordan. By the end of the season LaVine lead the league in points off the bench, showing off potential to be a star. Before the 2016-17 season the Timberwolves exercised the fourth-year team option. LaVine had a career high 40 points with seven threes in a loss against the Sacramento Kings. This season however ended in tragedy on February 4, 2017 when Lavine tore his ACL in his left knee and underwent surgery 10 days later.
